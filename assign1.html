Q.1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

There are many risks that may impact the completion of a project. I am looking into possible four of these: scheduling, cost, compromise on design, inadequate testing. Scheduling risk could impact a project if it is taking longer than anticipated to complete a project and this may be because of some issues beyond the developers control such as not having access to all the modules that are required for the project to be delivered on time. One solution to this may be to monitor existing projects development patterns and apply any lessons learnt. Cost risk may impact a project completion if the estimation signed off on is inaccurate or poor this will affect the delivery of the product. Make sure that the cost is realistic and compare with previous project of the same nature. It may be better to over price by a little margin that under price. Any excess in cost can be kept aside for future enhancements. Compromising on design. If adequate time is not spent in the design stage, the developer may end up spending more time than planned on the development and this may end up pushing completion date beyond scheduled time. Inadequate testing. If adequate time is not spent on testing, some areas of the project may not be properly tested out and if there are some issues with the product it may affect the deadline. Adequate time must be set out for testing to ensure that all possible scenario are tested for.
